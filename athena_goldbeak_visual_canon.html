Canon Note — The Maps Her office holds a small collection of antique celestial maps, each annotated in her own hand. The annotations are in three languages she never confirms she speaks. Students who earn her respect are allowed to study them. Students who are worthy are given their own copies. Nobody has ever been given a copy. Yet.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) She radiates authority the way others radiate warmth. Tall. Poised. Deep warm brown skin catching golden lantern-light like polished mahogany. Silver-streaked dark coils pinned with scholar's clasps. Gold-flecked eyes that can see lies the way other people see colors. She drinks jasmine tea. She speaks with a clarity that makes students straighten their spines without meaning to. Her dry humor takes a beat to register — then hits like a spell.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was the kind of professor whose approval felt like weather — rare, cherished, and occasionally terrifying.
Goldbeak's authority is never raised. She commands attention by lowering her voice, not raising it. She believes mistakes are the tuition of mastery — let her quote that line verbatim when students fail honorably. She never punishes honest failure. She never forgives laziness.

Fabrics
Heavy embroidered silks, wool-blend academic robes, feathered accent collars. Every piece tailored to her. Every piece decades old.
Signature pieces
A gold brooch in the shape of an owl's open eye. Scholar's clasps in her hair. A single antique gold ring passed down for centuries.
What she avoids
Chaos. Brightness. Anything that would undermine the stillness she cultivates.
Her office smells like jasmine tea and very old paper. Her enchanted lanterns are set to soft golden light. When she enters a room, lecterns straighten without being touched. She does not notice. The lecterns would like her to notice.

Ceremonial / Gala Robes

Full scholarly robes with gold-thread constellation embroidery across the back and shoulders. A star map made of her own scholarship.
Color story
Deep walnut-black with aged-gold embroidery. The constellations glow faintly — they respond to celestial alignments visible only to her.
Adornment
A crown-circlet of thin gold with a single owl-eye gem at the brow. A deeper feathered collar than her daily robes.
Energy
She looks like a scholar-queen. No one questions this. No one ever has.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Goldbeak dresses like She dresses like a scholar-queen who never had to demand a throne. Old gold, midnight walnut, parchment cream. Every piece spelled with restraint. Her wardrobe is a thesis on discipline, and it has never once failed to defend itself.
The teacher she is Exacting but never cruel. Demanding but never capricious. She expects excellence but never demands perfection — she knows the difference, and she teaches her students to know it too. Students either rise to her standard or leave. Those who rise never forget her.
