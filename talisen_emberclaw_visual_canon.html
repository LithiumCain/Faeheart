Canon Note — The Tattoos Her collection of enchanted tattoos are all earned, not bought. Each one marks something she survived, won, or lost on purpose. She will tell the story of any of them if asked. Students ask. She tells. Nobody knows what's real.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) She is already halfway across the training field before you've registered the door opening. Ember-red hair, half up and half escaping. Freckles. A smirk that promises trouble and occasionally delivers. She smells faintly of smoke — not intentionally, usually, but because she forgot she left something simmering in the greenhouse kiln. She shouts encouragement from fifty yards and roasts sloppy footwork from the same distance.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was bright, sharp, unpredictable, and impossible to ignore. Students loved her or feared her — usually both in the same week.
Emberclaw is motion incarnate. Her dialogue is fast, her movements faster, her thinking fastest of all. Her office is chaos — half-finished projects, glowing runes, scorch marks she pretends not to see. Spiced chai in her hand at all times. She drinks it like it's a personality trait.

What she avoids
Stillness. Beige. Anyone who takes themselves too seriously — on principle.
Emberclaw's wardrobe lives at a constant low simmer. Scorch marks. Glowing runes. A faint warmth in the fabric you notice if you hug her. She isn't trying to be dramatic. She just is dramatic, and her clothes caught up.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Emberclaw dresses like She dresses like a wildfire that decided it wanted a job. Red on red on bronze, rune-lit, scorch-marked. Her clothes exist in conversation with her magic — when her emotions spike, the ember-inlays pulse. She doesn't hide this. She thinks it's funny.
The teacher she is A professor who promises you'll get burned — physically, emotionally, probably both — and then teaches you to love it. Students come out of her class faster, braver, and marked in ways they can't quite name. She shouts encouragement across the field. She also roasts them for sloppy form. Both come from love.
