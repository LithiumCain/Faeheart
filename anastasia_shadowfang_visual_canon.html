Canon Note — The Chaotic Drawer Her office is immaculate — except for a single drawer full of broken weapon parts she swears she'll fix "one day." She has sworn this for years. The drawer has outlived three replacement cabinets. Students who earn her respect are allowed to look inside. Students who try to clean it are never allowed to look again.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) She walks in like she already knows every exit and has decided, charitably, not to need any of them. Pale skin. Dark hair smoothed back. Ice-grey eyes that move through a room the way her wolf moves through a field. She wears dark gloves — she says the metal dust from the weapons vault dries out her hands. She drinks iced coffee in winter. She can correct your stance with a gentle touch and put you flat on your back in the same movement, and both feel somehow like kindness.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was lethal in the way silk is lethal — beautiful until it closes around your throat, and then entirely beautiful.
Shadowfang is patient but not indulgent. She expects effort, not perfection. She is fiercely protective of underdogs and absolutely ruthless with show-offs. When she hums battle hymns under her breath while demonstrating, students learn to recognize the melodies — each one maps to a lesson.

Teaching Attire

Fabrics
Reinforced leather, cold-pressed canvas, dark-dyed wool. Nothing that swishes. Nothing that announces movement before it happens.
Signature pieces
Dark gloves. Always. A thin silver chain at the throat with a fang pendant. Twin daggers at the hips she has never once drawn in class and never needs to.
What she avoids
Warm colors. Sound. Anything that would soften her silhouette. She chose this blade edge on purpose.
She wears the same silhouette for fifty years and never looks dated. Shadowfang's style is timeless threat. When she hums while demonstrating, the classroom quiets without being asked.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Shadowfang dresses like She dresses like a woman who chose her silhouette when she was young and has not needed to update it. Pale steel, smoke black, muted gold. A wardrobe that tells students, wordlessly, that weapons are not the dangerous thing in this room.
The teacher she is Elegant. Lethal. Disarmingly calm. She'll flip a student onto the mat without breaking eye contact, help them up, and ask what they think they did wrong. She wants them to see the answer. She will never hand it to them.
