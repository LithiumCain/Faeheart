Prose-Ready Description
First impression — any POV Balayage blond hair that had clearly been tousled into its current state by someone who'd stopped caring about it around the third thought of the morning. Seaglass green eyes — bright and specific, the kind that found things to look at in every direction. Tan skin, warm, the same warmth as his sister. Lean and quick-looking, like someone who moved fast out of enthusiasm rather than urgency. He smiled at you before he'd decided to.
The twin recognition moment The eyes. That was always the giveaway — that particular shade of seaglass green, bright and vivid, the same on both of them. Everything else was different: where she was golden and warm, he was tousled and quick. But the eyes were the same. The proof that they came from the same place.
His scene energy — from the manuscript Crumbs on his shirt, a pastry in each hand, bouncing on the balls of his feet. "I'm carb-loading." "For what? Emotional endurance?" "Yes." That's Harrison. He means it. He's right.
Everyday Style
Campus / General Wear
Palette
Navy Cream Soft brown Warm tan Seaglass accent
Silhouette
Effortlessly preppy — fitted but not fussy, neat without being stiff. He looks put-together in the way someone does when their clothes actually fit and they simply don't overthink it.
Signature pieces
A well-loved bag that carries too many books. Layers he half-forgets he's wearing — an open overshirt, a jacket left in the wrong classroom. Always something slightly crumpled. The crumple is honest.
The scholar tell
Ink on his hands almost always. Occasionally on his cuff. He notices eventually. He doesn't particularly mind. The books were worth it.
Footwear
Good boots or clean casual — something he can walk fast in, because he always walks fast. He's always going somewhere interesting and running slightly late to get there.
What he avoids
Nothing deliberately. He just forgets things exist. He has owned the same three navy shirts in rotation for two years and sees no reason to complicate this.
Harrison's style reads as warm and collegiate because it is. Navy and cream on warm tan skin with tousled balayage blond hair is the visual equivalent of his personality — approachable, bright, slightly chaotic, fundamentally trustworthy. He looks like someone's favorite person. He usually is.
Formal / Ball Look Locked from Manuscript
The Lazuline Ball — Canon
Base coat
Midnight blue — deep, rich, the color of the sky between stars. A scholar's formal color. It suits him in a way that feels inevitable rather than deliberate.
Embroidery
Constellations in silver and gold filigree across the coat — intricate, exact, the kind of detail that rewards looking closely. He probably knew the name of every constellation depicted. He definitely told someone about it.
Cuffs
Glowing runes that curl around his wrists and hands like a loving caress — as written, locked. The magic responds to him. The scholarly prince aesthetic is complete and unironic.
Hair at ball
Soft ribbons of brown beneath a crescent-moon circlet — still him, still tousled, but the circlet gives it context. He looks like he belongs in this world. He does, more than he knows yet.
Sibling read
He and Allura step through the doors side by side — he in midnight constellation blue, she in stardust lavender and gold. They look like they were made for this world. They were carved from the same starlit culture. The ballroom reads them as royalty before anyone knows their names.
His energy
Elegant, composed, born for this world — as written. He wears formality the way he wears everything else: like he didn't overthink it, and it all just worked.
The ball entrance is a visual thesis statement for both twins. He's the scholarly prince to her force of nature. Together they read as something that belongs here — which is exactly the dramatic irony, because neither of them knows yet what they truly are.
Sibling Dynamic — Visual Language
How Allura reads
Golden, celestial, the force of nature. She arrives and the space adjusts. She's the one people look at first and keep coming back to.
How Harrison reads
Warm, approachable, the one who makes friends. He arrives and the room relaxes. He's the one people talk to first — and then wonder later why they told him so much.
Together Side by side, they're a complete picture — the twin who blazes and the twin who draws you in. Same seaglass eyes, same warm skin, completely different gravitational fields. He's the reason she has friends. She's the reason he survives his own curiosity. They have always been each other's counterweight.
