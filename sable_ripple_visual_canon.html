Canon Note — The Floating Calligraphy Her office is minimalist except for a wall of floating calligraphy that shifts with her mood. Students who know her well can read her emotional weather by the letterforms before she speaks. She has never confirmed what the calligraphy says. She has never denied it, either.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) She speaks softly and the whole classroom leans forward. Pale skin with a faint lavender undertone. Long dark hair like ink suspended in water. Deep violet-plum eyes that settle on you for a beat longer than you expect — and then she delivers a single, devastatingly accurate piece of advice. Her classroom smells faintly of ink and rain. A soft glow wraps her hands when she shapes a spell. She is gentle. She is not fragile.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was the kind of calm that made loud people go quiet without knowing why.
Ripple's dialogue is spare, quiet, and surgical. She drinks lavender tea. She keeps a small bowl of river stones she rearranges when thinking — students learn that when the stones move, something important is about to be said. Her power isn't showy. It's listened to.

Signature pieces
A thin silver ink-quill pendant. Tiny pearl studs. Ink-ripple embroidery at the sleeves that moves when she does.
What she avoids
Anything sharp or loud. She doesn't wear gold. She doesn't wear red. Both would disrupt the register she teaches in.
Ripple's style secret is sustained low frequency. Her clothes whisper. Her magic whispers. Her voice whispers. And everyone listens harder because of it.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Ripple dresses like She dresses like someone who knows silence is more powerful than ornament. Watered silk, ink ripple, soft violet. Illusion-woven fabric that responds to magic the way her hair responds to wind. Her wardrobe is listening, and so are her students.
The teacher she is Specialist in illusions, spell-flow, and emotional resonance. She never raises her voice. She never needs to. Gentle but not fragile, calm but not passive. Students adore her because she sees them — exactly, patiently, without flinching.
