Canon Note — The Locked Cabinet His office is immaculate — except for a single locked cabinet no one has ever seen opened. Students have theories. Faculty have theories. Even Banister has theories. Ambrose finds this delightful and has chosen never to confirm or deny any of them. The cabinet has been there longer than most of the staff.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) Charming, unsettling, and entirely too amused by his own reputation. Tall. Elegant. Pale skin, dark hair swept back with aristocratic precision. Pale grey-blue eyes that glint with private amusement. Subtle sanguine sigils at his wrists — blood-magic glyphs that pulse faintly when he laughs. He wears dark tailored coats. He twirls a silver pen when he's bored. He smells of cloves and old books. He teaches dangerous magic with the casual confidence of a man who knows exactly how dangerous he is.
Neutral / third person shorthand He was dramatic, witty, and deeply invested in student safety — three qualities he combined into a teaching style that felt, reliably, like a well-edited duel.
Ambrose is theatrical but not reckless. He maintains a strict boundary between humor and actual danger. He'll pause mid-lecture to make sure students appreciate a moment, then snap into surgical precision the second anyone's safety is at stake. His dialogue should purr. His warnings should land.

Signature pieces

What he avoids
Warm colors. Casual wear. Anything that could be described as approachable. He chose this register on purpose.
Ambrose wears his aesthetic like a spell — it holds the lecture hall in a register students don't know they've agreed to. When he rolls up a sleeve to demonstrate sanguine work, the room goes still. That's the point. He never abuses it.

Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Ambrose dresses like He dresses like a man who read a lot of gothic novels in his youth and decided to keep the aesthetic. Wine dark, ink black, dusty rose. Sanguine glyphs as design. His wardrobe is a running joke he is in on. Students learn this by year two. By year three, they've stolen the aesthetic themselves.
The teacher he is An exceptional teacher: clear, precise, deeply invested in student safety. Behind the theatricality is a man who has never lost a student, and does not intend to start. His ethics of power course changes people. Quietly. Permanently.
