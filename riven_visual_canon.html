Lycari Form Terminology Species Canon
Faecoat
The two-legged form. Fae in appearance — but with wolf ears always present, always visible. The coat they wear in the world of courts and universities and other people's expectations.
Riven coined it. Or at least he says he did. Kaelith says he heard it somewhere and forgot.
Furcoat
Full wolf form. Four legs, full shift, true nature. What they are when nothing is watching and the forest has room for them. Also what Riven calls it when he wants to make Kaelith's eye twitch.
The more formal Lycari term may exist — but among the Stormveil pack, it's furcoat. Has been for years.
The Ears — Lycari Canon In faecoat form, wolf ears are always present. Always. They are not glamoured, not hidden, not suppressed. They are part of what the Lycari are — the one thing that doesn't change between forms. They move with emotion: flat against the skull in anger or grief, perked forward in attention or delight, angled back in warning. For Riven specifically, his ears are expressive in ways his face sometimes isn't. Pay attention to them. They tell the truth even when he doesn't.
Shifting Progression — Year 1 (Book 1) The Stormveil pack are in Year 1 at SNU. Currently: faecoat or furcoat only — no in-between. The bipedal wolf form, partial shifts, claw-holds, face features — all of that comes with training across Years 2 and 3. Right now they are either fully themselves or fully the wolf. There is no middle ground yet. That limitation matters narratively.

Furcoat Form Canon
Primary Coloring
Warm gold — the color of late afternoon sun through amber glass. Matches his faecoat hair precisely. Like the wolf and the fae were designed to be the same thing, just wearing different shapes.
Secondary / Shadow
Cream undermarkings — chest, legs, face panels. Soft contrast, nothing harsh. The overall read is golden warmth, not dramatic contrast. He looks like sunshine given teeth.
Wolf Form Notes
Size
Large — visibly pack-size, bigger than ordinary wolves. Slightly smaller than Reign in furcoat. Still enormous by any normal standard.
Movement
Where Reign moves quiet, Riven moves fast. He covers ground with reckless joy. Running with him feels like being chased by good news.
Manuscript note
Allura spots him as the "golden" wolf in the treeline — warm summer day coloring. She clocks him before she sees Reign. He's impossible to miss.
Everyday Style — Faecoat
The Vibe Loud. Maximalist. He dresses like he's the main event everywhere he goes — and somehow he's usually right. Where Reign's warmth is accidental, Riven's is a weapon he wields with full awareness and zero shame. He layers things that shouldn't work together and they work. Kaelith has stopped asking how.

Signature
Something unexpected every time — a fur-trimmed edge, an embroidered detail, a color that shouldn't be in the outfit but anchors it. He has one piece that always raises an eyebrow. He considers this a success.

Locked from the reference image — layered chains of varying lengths, mixed metals, small pendants. Not precious. Personal. The difference matters to him even if he'd joke about it.
What he avoids
Nothing. Riven avoids nothing. This is the problem. This is also the point.
The contrast that defines Riven's style vs Reign's: Reign's warmth is background radiation — you absorb it slowly. Riven's warmth hits you like opening a furnace door. Same palette family. Completely different volume. When they're in a room together, Riven draws the eye first. Reign is the one you can't stop coming back to.
Formal / Ball Look
The Lazuline Ball — Canon
Energy
He dressed up. He fully committed. He spent more time than Reign did and will absolutely deny it. He looks like a prince and he knows it and he said so out loud.
Base
Deep gold or warm amber — something that catches the ballroom light and gives it back doubled. He is not wearing dark tones to a party. That is Reign's territory.
Details
More ornamentation than Reign, less than the most elaborate fae in the room. He hits the sweet spot between effort and excess with the precision of someone who has practiced this exactly.
The mooncrown
Reign's blood-forged crescent moon piece — shards radiating outward like a star map, faint inner glow. Riven wears it like he was born in it. He was. Reign knew he would be. That's why he made it that way.
Hair at ball
Still chaotic, but intentionally chaotic. The platinum catches the lanternlight. He adjusted it approximately forty times. He'd tell you he didn't touch it.
Stormveil Pack — Visual Contrast
The three of them together Reign is shadow and warmth — dark clothes, golden-brown, quiet menace. Riven is light and noise — platinum, gold, already laughing. Kaelith is the precise middle — dark hair, wolfish angles, watching both of them with an expression that says she loves them and they exhaust her. Together they read as a unit instantly. Three wolves who grew up in the same storm.
