Canon Note — The Mismatched Socks He always wears mismatched socks. Always. Students have tested this with increasingly thorough surveillance. The answer is consistent and there is no pattern. When asked, he shrugs — "Pair-matching doesn't fit in the schedule." Students who understand the schedule he is referring to (corrupted lands, magical anomalies, the list he keeps that will not fit on any desk) stop asking. Students who don't understand yet are invited to keep watching.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) He looks like he hasn't slept since the Ashfall Betrayal and is not planning to start. Tall, sandy-blond, habitually rumpled. Warm hazel-green eyes that are tired and kind and — if you look closely, which most students don't in week one — sharp. He drinks tea like it's a coping mechanism. His socks don't match. He delivers Convergence Theory with the quiet confidence of a man who has personally witnessed most of the anomalies on the syllabus. He's not mad. He's not disappointed. He's just confused. And trying his best.
Neutral / third person shorthand He looked like a man who had accepted chaos as a career and was handling it with tea, sighs, and surprisingly excellent pedagogy.
Wexley's voice is Glen Powell calm with a quiet undertow. Dry humor. Soft sighs. The signature cadence is "Okay. Okay. So…" followed by him completely absorbing a magical catastrophe he was not prepared for. He's the only professor who understands magical anomalies, and the only one who doesn't panic easily. When Reign's daggers explode, Wexley just says: "Please don't bleed on anything. It stains the runes." He takes Reign because he has to. The list only gets longer.

What he avoids
Alarm. Panic. Formality. Matching socks. Any of the above would misrepresent the person he has chosen to be.
Wexley's wardrobe is controlled exhaustion. Every piece functional, every piece comfortable, every piece slightly softer than it should be because he's been wearing it for years. When a first-year sees him in a corridor and doesn't realize he's the professor, that's not an accident — it's camouflage. He likes being underestimated. It gives him time to think.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Wexley dresses like He dresses like the smartest man in the room who has decided, as a policy, not to look like it. Dusk sage, tea brown, old paper. The wardrobe of a scholar who has read too much and been right too often, and has learned to soften the edges so students don't run. It works. Until it doesn't — and then the edges are exactly where he needed them all along.
The teacher he is Convergence Theory: the science of how magic interacts with species, runes, and fate. He is the only professor who understands magical anomalies at the depth the current moment requires. He is also the only one who doesn't panic easily. When everyone else is shouting about Reign's blood daggers, Wexley is already adding it to the list. The list is long. He has capacity for more. He always has.
