Prose-Ready Description
First impression (Reign's POV) Golden, windtossed hair that catches lanternlight like something lit from within — long and layered, the ends pale as the moon. Warm, honey-kissed skin. Eyes the color of starfire: vivid, luminous green that dazzles like she's carrying light she doesn't know she has. Soft cheekbones, a long torso, strong presence in a frame that doesn't announce itself — it simply arrives.
Neutral / third person shorthand She had the kind of warmth that read as sunlight — golden hair, golden skin, eyes too green and too bright for someone who claimed not to believe in magic.
When you're stuck mid-scene, "golden" is your anchor word for Allura. Hair, skin, the way light finds her — gold keeps coming back because it's who she is before she knows it.
Everyday Style
Campus / General Wear
Silhouette
Fitted but never fussy. Things that move with her body — nothing that fights her. She doesn't perform getting dressed.
Palette
Deep gold Deep navy Midnight Soft white
Fabrics
Nothing stiff. Soft wovens, draped things, the kind of fabric that hints at shape rather than demanding it.
Signature pieces
A well-worn cloak she pulls tighter out of habit, not cold. Boots that have been places. Usually a layer she can shed.
What she avoids
Over-ornamentation. Anything that announces she's trying. She can look stunning in a navy fitted top and dark trousers and she knows it.
Field / Survival Days
Silhouette
Same fitted instinct, but more practical. Nothing loose enough to catch. She ties her hair back but pieces always escape.
Details
Cloak still present. Belt or wrap at the waist — she likes something anchoring her center. Dark trousers, fitted boots. Gold or ivory on top.
Energy
She looks like she dressed for class and ended up in the forest. It still works.
Her everyday style secret: the colors do the heavy lifting. Deep navy + gold reads as effortlessly celestial. She doesn't need embellishment — the palette is the statement.
Formal / Ball Look
The Lazuline Ball Gown — Scene Canon
Silhouette
Floor-length. Full skirt, fitted bodice. The kind of gown that doesn't need a train to be dramatic — it moves with her instead of behind her.
Color story
Deepest midnight black at the center of the bodice, melting outward through rich deep navy into soft lavender at the skirt edges. Every hem burns with bright gold. The transition is seamless — like watching a night sky catch sunrise.
Fabric
Goldflecked tulle over the skirt — suspended stardust effect. The bodice has weight and structure but the skirt breathes. Each movement sends the gold flecks shimmering.
Embellishment
Gold droplets suspended from the fabric like frozen stardust caught mid-fall. Not clustered — scattered, deliberate, like a constellation someone mapped by hand.
Neckline
High enough to be formal, low enough to show the collarbone where her runes sit. The runes are part of the look whether she wants them to be or not.
Arms
Exposed, or lightly draped — the rune patterns on her arms need to be visible for the lantern-flare moment. This is not optional. The gown was made for that reveal even if she didn't know it.
Ball — Hair
Style
Mostly down — her hair is too distinctive to hide. Soft movement, some pieces swept back or pinned loosely. The length and layers do the work.
Crown / adornment
A crown of yellow and pink flowers — as written and locked. Warm, soft, almost wildflower-casual against the formality of the gown. That contrast is intentional. She's not performing royalty. She just arrived.
Texture
Lanternlight turns her hair into soft fire. This is a recurring image — use it. Gold hair + gold light = she looks lit from inside.
Ball — Accessories
Jewelry
Minimal and deliberate. The gown is already a statement. Small gold earrings — drops or studs, nothing that competes with the flower crown. No necklace — the collarbone and runes are the necklace.
Hands
Bare. When Reign takes her hand on the dance floor, there's nothing between his palm and hers. That contact is sacred — don't interrupt it with rings or gloves.
Shoes
Gold or ivory — something that disappears under the skirt and only appears in motion. She doesn't draw attention to her feet. She draws attention to the way she moves.
The Rune Moment — Ball Scene When the lantern glow sweeps across her skin, the runes along her arms and collarbone flare in soft gold — intricate, ancient, not cast by her. Light reacting to her. The glow fades shy and fleeting. This is the first time anyone in the ballroom sees them light. Reign's stomach drops. This is the moment he knows something is more than he thought. The gown's bare arms are not a style choice — they're a narrative necessity.
When writing the ball scene from any POV: the gown reads differently in motion than standing still. Standing — she's striking. Moving — she becomes something else entirely. The gold-flecked tulle only reveals itself when she walks. Let the scene earn the fabric.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Allura dresses like She dresses like someone who never needed to try — and is slowly realizing that the things she reaches for instinctively (gold, midnight, things that move with her) are not coincidences. Her style is celestial without being performative. It suits a girl who grew up not knowing what she was, reaching for colors and silhouettes that echo a magic she hasn't named yet.
The contrast that defines her Flower crown over a stargown. Worn cloak over something beautiful. Soft face, strong presence. She carries contradiction without resolving it — and that's exactly right for who she is in Book 1. She hasn't resolved herself yet either.
