Canon Note — The Silence He is the only wolf alive who can silence Reign's wolf with a single word. Not a command. Not a raised voice. A single quiet syllable spoken from decades of discipline and unspeakable grief. The wolf obeys because the wolf recognizes. There are reasons for that. Vaelor has never told them.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) He does not enter a room. He arrives in one. Tall, lean, predatory. Black hair streaked with silver — not aged, marked. Molten amber eyes ringed in frost, the kind of eyes that have looked at grief for so long the grief has started looking back. A long dark coat that shifts like it's alive. Hands steady even when his voice isn't. He is striking, not pretty. Students don't fear him. They respect him. Wolves obey him.
Neutral / third person shorthand He carried the weight of a kingdom on his shoulders and the weight of a grave in his chest — and somehow still had room in his hands to steady a student's stance.
Vaelor's voice is mellow, ancient, quietly dominant. Not Snape's cruelty. Not Gandalf's warmth. Something in between — dry humor, soft-spoken authority, lethal calm. He speaks in sentences that could cut glass without ever raising his voice. When he finally does raise it, the scene has already changed forever.

Heavy wool-blend coats, dark silks, reinforced leather at the shoulders. Every piece old. Every piece earned. Nothing new has been bought in decades.
Signature pieces
A small frost-wolf clasp at the throat — his mate's, and he has never taken it off. A single silver ring on his smallest finger. Boots that have walked corrupted lands and returned.
What he avoids
Color. Decoration. Anything that would ask to be looked at. His presence is the only statement he will permit.
His coat shifts in rooms with no wind. Students have written theses about why. The answer is simpler and sadder than any of them have guessed — it's not magic. It's the bond that never healed, still breathing through the fabric she made for him.
The Weight He Carries
Ceremonial / Council Robes
Silhouette
Full formal coat, floor-length, with a high wolf-mantle collar of deep silver-black fur. Structured shoulders. Severe silhouette. The shape of an alpha who renounced his title and kept the bearing anyway.
Color story
Midnight ink with amber threadwork tracing the spine and cuffs — the only warmth he allows himself to wear. The wolf-mantle reads as smoke in one light, frost in another.
Adornment
The frost-wolf clasp at the throat, visible. A thin silver circlet he wears only when officiating primal ceremonies — a crown he never claimed, worn on the nights the old bloodline demands acknowledgment.
Energy
He looks like a king who refused the throne and became something ancient instead. No one in the room outranks him. No one tries.
What He Carries (And Does Not Say) Born Vaelor Stormfang, twin to the Stormfang King. The last uncorrupted alpha of the old bloodline. His Auric-aligned mate died trying to save the royal pups — Reign, Riven, Kaelith — and the shattered bond is a wound that never healed. He renounced his title, left his pack, and became a guardian of primal magic. He found Reign first. He has raised him quietly from the shadows ever since. He has never told Reign why.
Vaelor is the tragedy of "what happens when a bond breaks but the love remains." Book 1: calm, controlled, respected — and beginning to sense the corruption returning. Book 2: cracks show, the wolf destabilizes, Zor arrives and notices. Book 3: he becomes the Rotborne. Not evil. Broken. Reign and Zor must end him. His last act is begging them to. His death brings the king to SNU.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Vaelor dresses like He dresses like an alpha who renounced his crown and kept the bearing. Midnight, amber, silver frost. Coats that move like they remember someone. His wardrobe is a quiet vow he made to his mate, to his nephews, to the young wolves he stands between and the wilds. Every piece severe. Every piece loved. Every piece a kind of prayer.
The teacher he is The unspoken protector of every young wolf on campus. Teaches grounding, discipline, wolf-mind control, primal resonance. He is the canary in the coal mine — first to sense corruption returning — and the man who quietly raised Reign from the shadows. He loves that boy more fiercely than he will ever admit. He is the reason Reign has not yet become what the king became.
