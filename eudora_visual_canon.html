Canon Note — The Cabin Her home is woven from willow vines and moonlit branches. White flowers glow along the path. The door opens before visitors knock. The cabin is not haunted. It is alive, and it answers to her. The forest brings her what it wants her to find — abandoned pups, wounded fae, old griefs looking for a place to settle. She has never once turned any of them away.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (Prologue POV — Nisara & Rey) The door opens before they knock. She stands barefoot on the threshold, silver braid trailing like smoke. Her shawl is stitched with bark and bone. The air around her pulses with quiet, ancient magic. Storm-washed stone for eyes. A voice soft enough to hush a forest — and heavy enough to name three pups Stormveil and have the world agree.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was the kind of old that made weather apologize for arriving. Barefoot, silver-braided, stone-eyed, and utterly unsurprised by anything.
Eudora's voice is sparse and final. She never wastes syllables. When she speaks, the forest listens. Don't give her monologues — give her sentences that land like stones dropped into water. Her best lines are the ones characters remember years later.

The silver braid. A small carved antler charm at the throat. A belt of woven vine she has never replaced. The bark-and-bone shawl, always.
Feet
Bare. Indoors, outdoors, in snow. Always bare. The forest knows her footprints.
When She Leaves The Woods
Silhouette
A long grey cloak, hood up. The silver braid tucked inside. The stone-grey eyes become the only visible thing.
Details
She carries a staff carved from a tree she knew by name. No jewelry beyond the antler charm. No ornament that announces her.
Energy
She looks like weather. She is treated like weather. People who should know better make way for her without realizing they've moved.
Eudora never changes her clothes for the scene. The scene adjusts to her. When she walks into a throne room barefoot in her bark shawl, that room is now a bark-shawl room.
The Prologue Moment
The Naming Moment — Prologue Canon
Setting
Blood moon. Fog-banked forest. A willow-vine cabin lit by glowing white flowers.
Action
She cradles three wolf pups against her chest, humming under her breath. The middle pup's eyes flash gold as lightning cracks the sky. A rare flower unfurls in the archway out of season.
Line
Stormborn. Spoken once. And the world rearranges to accommodate it.
The Name
"I will give them a name to keep them hidden. Stormveil. The storm that brought them here will shield what they are until they choose their own names."
Aftermath
The door closes. The forest exhales. Somewhere, a howl fractures into silence. Nothing is ever the same again. Eudora does not look back.
What She Knows (And Does Not Say) Eudora knows exactly what Reign is. She has known since the blood moon. She knows what Allura is, too — or will be — and she has known since the first time the Stormveils mentioned her scent. She has never once told them. She is waiting for them to choose their own names. That's the only way it works. That's the only way it ever worked.
The prologue sets her tone forever. Every Eudora scene going forward should echo that first one: magic that responds to her without announcement, words that land like law, bare feet on whatever surface should not support her. She is the anchor of the series mythology. Write her that way.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Eudora dresses like She dresses like someone who belongs to the forest and has never tried to dress like anything else. The wardrobe is not rustic — it is rooted. There is a difference, and that difference is three centuries of being the only person the woods would open their door for.
The contrast that defines her Ancient and tender. Terrifyingly wise and genuinely warm. She raised three wolf pups into a pack with her own hands and taught them to love each other before she taught them what they were. She is fierce and protective and the Stormveils call her home.
