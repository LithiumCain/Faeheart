Canon Note — The Mint Leaves He chews mint leaves constantly — he says it keeps his senses sharp. Students suspect (correctly) it's to cover the scent of greenhouse smoke from late-night herb experiments. He denies this with a straight face. Every time.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) Young — young enough that half the first-years mistake him for an upperclassman, once. Never twice. Deep warm brown skin. Short dark curls. Sharp amber eyes that track movement before the movement finishes. He chews mint leaves and speaks quietly and doesn't posture — he demonstrates. His wolf form is sleek and dark and quiet enough to make grown predators reconsider their plans.
Neutral / third person shorthand He looked like a wolf who had decided to try being a teacher, and was being extremely patient about it.
Moonfang is the professor most likely to spar one-on-one with students who need confidence more than critique. His understated humor lands devastatingly when it lands. He keeps a sketchbook full of landscapes and students mid-motion, and he denies being an artist. He is an artist.

What he avoids
Anything formal. Anything that announces him. He is young enough that announcing himself would undercut what he's building.
He is young for a professor and he knows it. He works twice as hard to prove he deserves the title — without ever saying he's working twice as hard. His students pick it up by osmosis and fight for him because of it.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Moonfang dresses like He dresses like a combat instructor who never stopped being a student. Functional. Earned. Quiet. The wardrobe of a young professor who refuses to fake seniority and chose instead to let the work speak.
The teacher he is Competitive in a quiet way — raising an eyebrow instead of boasting. The kind of professor who will spar with you at dawn, correct your stance without a word, and then buy you a cup of something hot when your hands are shaking. His students grow up fast. None of them grow up alone.
