Culture Canon · Book 1 Reference · Faeheart World Bible
Identity Canon-Locked

Selenari Nexus is the premier institution of magical and cultural learning in Xiphos — and it is unlike any other academy in the realm. It was not built; it was grown. Its halls are woven from living trees whose trunks the architects trained across centuries into spires. Its walls are moonstone quarried from deep beneath the forest floor. Its walkways are stone paths laced with magic that glow softly underfoot after dusk. Students who wake in the middle of the night to use the bathhouse have described the experience as walking through a constellation.

It is the only school in Xiphos that accepts students from every Faekin lineage: fae of the courts, Lycari pack-trios, Stryxian scholar-hatchlings, dragon-bonded recruits, the rare Wild Fae whose enclave has sent them out to be witnessed by the wider world. Each species has a dorm wing, but classes mix freely. Pack Leadership is taught beside Font Mastery. Dragon-Egg Observation is down the corridor from Spellsong. Advanced Tracking meets in a grove that, depending on the week, might host an Ember-Font workshop instead because the grove itself has opinions.

Most students attend for three years. The academy calendar is split into three Arcs per year. Most bond their mates here. Most awaken their Font here. Most, by the time they graduate, have been changed so completely they are no longer the person their family sent.

The Nexus is not a school, exactly. The Nexus is a crucible. You come in convinced you know who you are. You leave holding whoever has survived the second year. — Banister Whitis, Headmaster's welcome speech, every year
The Hidden Campus Canon-Locked

Selenari Nexus is hidden in the Ember Scale Wilds, a deep primeval forest roughly equidistant from Nulune, Ameliora, and Kaios. The campus cannot be reached by simply walking toward it. The path reveals itself only to those who have been invited — letters arrive the way letters always arrive in Xiphos, by enchanted parchment, and the parchment itself guides the student the final stretch.

Seen from above, the Nexus is a concentric ring city. Three great circular paths spiral outward from a central grove where a crystalline spire complex rises through the canopy — the magical heart of the campus, visible for leagues at night. The inner ring houses the oldest classrooms and the Great Library. The middle ring holds the dorms, the bathhouse, the dining halls, the practice groves. The outer ring — wide and wooded — is the training perimeter, blending seamlessly into the Ember Scale Wilds beyond.

The violet-pink stone paths that link the rings glow with a soft magic-light at dusk. Students joke that the paths decide which direction they want you to go. The faculty do not contradict this.

The campus is alive. Trees that form classroom walls must be asked before they are pruned. The bathhouse adjusts its water temperature based on who is bathing. The library rearranges its shelves overnight — students have learned to bookmark by scent rather than location. The crystalline spire at the center is said to pulse in time with the Twin Moons when they align. None of this is controlled by the faculty. All of this is tolerated because the alternative is fighting the Nexus, and nobody has won that fight yet.
The Four Factions Cultural Canon

Every student at Selenari Nexus is sorted — not by species, not by Font, not by class, but by philosophical alignment. The sorting happens quietly, during the first week. Students do not choose their faction; the faction chooses them. Most Nexus graduates keep their faction affiliation for life and continue to network with fellow members well into their careers.

Faction I
Grove of Surprises
Experimental magic theorists. Break rules on purpose. Publish findings the courts never asked for. The Grove is where the interesting chaos comes from.
Faction II
Circle of Guardians
Magical law enforcers. The Nexus's internal order, and the people who graduate into cross-species diplomatic work. Don't break rules near them.
Faction III
Order of Promise
Scholars of prophecy, oath, and binding. The quietest faction. Most meet privately and keep notes the rest of the campus cannot decipher.
Faction IV
Grove of Enlightened Stars
Astral historians. Celestial readers. Moon-mappers. Most of the Stryxian students end up here. So do most of the dragon-bonded. So does Harrison.

The four factions are not rival houses in the Hogwarts sense. They are lenses. A student of the Grove of Surprises might sit beside a Circle of Guardians at dinner and argue cheerfully about the ethics of the Font choice they both just made. The factions matter less than the Scholar's Oath, and the Oath is the only thing at the Nexus that every student swears.

The Scholar's Oath Canon-Locked

Every student at Selenari Nexus swears the Scholar's Oath on the first day of their first Arc. The Oath binds them to their chosen field of study for life. It cannot be revoked. It cannot be transferred. It cannot be faked — the magic of the Oath runs through the moonstone foundation of the campus itself, and a student who swears falsely will find their magic refuse them for the next seven days while the Nexus decides what to do about it.

I swear to pursue knowledge with care. I swear to wield power with consent. I swear to remember that what I learn here will one day walk beside me, even when I do not know it is there. — The Scholar's Oath · spoken at the Great Library

Students touch the cover of The Forgotten Scholars — the burnt-leather book chronicling the Ashfall Betrayal — upon entering the library for the first time. It is a silent vow. Never again.

The Ashfall Betrayal Historical Canon

Decades ago, a cohort of rogue scholars attempted to rewrite the Convergence Theory — the academy's foundational magical doctrine holding that magic, fate, and history are inextricably interlinked. Their goal was nothing less than altering magic itself. The attempt succeeded only in destroying whole wings of the campus as students fought over reality-altering texts. Many died. Much was lost. The books they tried to rewrite were burned. The survivors rebuilt the campus from its moonstone bones upward, and instituted the Scholar's Oath to prevent anything like it from ever happening again.

The only surviving record is The Forgotten Scholars, a book bound in burnt leather kept in the library. Every student touches its cover upon entering the library for the first time. A silent pledge never to repeat history.

Nobody alive at the Nexus today was present for the Betrayal. Banister Whitis was a student in the decade that followed. He has never spoken about that period. When asked directly, he changes the subject with a particular kind of smile that tells students not to ask again.

The Convergence Theory Foundational Doctrine

The central magical doctrine of Selenari Nexus. Holds that magic, fate, and history are inextricably interlinked — that every spell cast echoes forward and backward through time, that every bond formed reshapes the future, that every choice made in magical context ripples across the realm in ways no single caster can trace.

The Convergence Theory is why the Nexus exists in its current form. It is why the Scholar's Oath is absolute. It is why tampering with foundational texts — as the Ashfall rogues did — is the gravest crime a Nexus scholar can commit. The Theory holds that magic is not technology; magic is relationship. To rewrite magic is to rewrite what the world is.

First-year students learn the Theory's basics in Convergence Theory I. Upper years take advanced seminars. Only the Order of Promise teaches the deepest layer, and what they teach they do not write down.

Tavern Rumors & Half-Truths

What students whisper in the dormitory halls. What alumni say about the campus when they've had too much to drink. What the faculty deliberately don't correct. Some are accurate. Some are absurd. Some are exactly the kind of rumor that turns out, years later, to have been the truth all along.

The Library moves. I don't mean shelves. I mean the library. My third-year roommate swore she walked in one morning and the east wing was now the north wing. She checked the maps. The maps had changed. — Upper-year Grove of Surprises student
Don't swear the Scholar's Oath hungover. I did. I couldn't cast for nine days. Nine days. The Nexus knew. — Circle of Guardians alumna, three decades later, still grumbling
There's a room in the Great Library that only opens for students the Nexus thinks are in danger. Nobody teaches you where it is. You just find it. You just find it on the night you needed to. — Order of Promise student, in confidence
The Spire pulses in time with the Twin Moons. Everyone knows that. What they don't tell you is that it's been pulsing off-rhythm for the last two years. Getting faster. Faculty won't talk about it. — Stryxian first-year, possibly observant
Every seven years someone bonds their mate in the bathhouse. I'm not joking. It's happened reliably since the Nexus was built. The water knows. The bathhouse chooses the pair. Ask any of the groundskeepers. — Lyrianna, at a very drunk Grove of Surprises party
The Wildlands perimeter has a tree that speaks. One tree. It answers questions. You have to bring it something sweet — a fruit, a memory, a song. And you have to accept its answer. And you have to never, ever tell anyone else what it said. — Field Survival upper-year, deeply serious
There's a faction nobody talks about. A fifth one. They meet in the Spire on moonless nights. They don't have a name. They don't take new members. If you're meant to be one of them, they find you. — Almost certainly a joke. Almost.

Iron Canon — What Cannot Change

The rules below shape every SNU scene across the academy arc (Books 1-3, with setup continuing through Book 6). The Nexus is alive. The Nexus is deliberate. The Nexus is older than the staff, and all of them know it.

The Seven Iron Rules

1. Grown from living trees and moonstone. Hidden in the Ember Scale Wilds. Paths reveal themselves only to the invited.

2. The campus is alive. Trees must be asked. The library rearranges. The Spire pulses with the Twin Moons.

3. Three-year program, three Arcs per year. Every species studies alongside every other. Classes mix freely.

4. Every student swears the Scholar's Oath on Day One. It cannot be faked. The Nexus knows.

5. Four factions: Grove of Surprises, Circle of Guardians, Order of Promise, Grove of Enlightened Stars. Students do not choose — they are chosen.

6. Seven male faculty, seven female faculty, one headmaster. Numerology is deliberate. Seats noticed when vacant.

7. The Ashfall Betrayal is the academy's foundational trauma. Every student touches The Forgotten Scholars on first library entry. Never again.
