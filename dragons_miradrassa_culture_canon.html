Dragons
& The Realm of Miradrassa
"A dragon is not a beast you ride. A dragon is weather you have learned to walk beside."

Identity Canon-Locked

Dragons are not a species so much as a force of nature given a body. Each one hatches from an egg that has waited — sometimes years, sometimes centuries — for the right person to draw close enough to crack it. They do not breed in herds. They do not fly in formations. They do not raise warriors. They arrive, one at a time, when the world bends correctly.

A dragon-bonded fae is called many things across Xiphos — wyrm-walker, ember-kin, skybound — but in Miradrassa they are simply called bonded. The bond is the only title that matters. Everything else is regional flavor.

Bonded pairs travel the world. They take contracts the way Witcher monsters do — solitary, paid, dangerous, often misunderstood. They never ride in armies. They never serve a crown. The Skyborn Accord, written in dragon-blood after the Sundering, forbids it. Dragons cannot hold seats of power. They are too old, too vast, and too prone to outliving every government they touch.

The dragon does not ride for kings. The dragon does not bow to crowns. The dragon arrives, the dragon answers, and the dragon leaves before the dust has settled. — First verse of the Skyborn Accord
Miradrassa — The Homeland Canon-Locked

Miradrassa is the only place in Xiphos a dragon-bonded pair can call home. It is not a city. It is not a school. It is not a kingdom. It is a volcanic ridge complex reachable only by dragon flight, where the bonded return to rest, to heal, to grieve, and to listen.

The terrain itself is impossible. Black-glass cliffs rise from rivers of slow-cooled basalt. Vents breathe sulphur and warm ash that smells, somehow, like cedar and old smoke. Hot springs run obsidian-dark and salt-bright at the same time. Lava-fed forges glow blue-white in caves no fae could survive without a dragon's breath at their back.

Miradrassa is alive. The first ten dragons to bond, ten thousand years ago, chose at the end of their thousand-year span to become land. Their bodies are now the obsidian ridges, the lava-fed forges, the warm springs, the cliffs themselves. The land remembers them. Bonded pairs returning home are walking on the literal remains of their elders. The Ember Hearth — the great central hall of Miradrassa — sits inside what was once the chest cavity of the eldest of the ten. The hearth-fire has burned, without going out, for ten thousand years.

Miradrassa was built by fae hands and dragon breath together — but its foundation is older than fae civilization. The bonded do not build over their elders. They build with them. Stairs are carved into spines. Halls are hollowed between ribs. The young learn the names of the ten by walking through them.

The Bond Canon-Locked

A dragon bond is not chosen. It is not earned. It is not, in the traditional sense, summoned. The egg simply knows, and when its person comes near enough, it cracks.

Some eggs hatch the moment they are laid eyes upon. Most wait — quietly, dormant, sometimes for decades — until the right resonance walks past. A bond can happen at age six or age twenty-six. It can happen mid-class, mid-meal, mid-sentence. It cannot be predicted. It cannot be forced. It cannot be denied.

At Selenari Nexus, eggs are kept in the care of Professor Niraphael Cypress, the current Egg-Caller — a dragon-bonded fae whose particular gift is summoning dormant eggs to wake, and bringing them within range of the students who might be theirs. She has held the position for so long that nobody at the Nexus is entirely sure when she began. She declines to clarify.

Wolves cannot bond dragons. This is not custom. It is biology. The Lycari soul is too wolf-bright, too pack-bound, too earth-rooted for the sky-fire of a dragon to anchor in it. Bonded pairs are always fae. Always. Across ten thousand years of recorded bonding, no exception has ever held.
An egg that keeps appearing in the wrong room is not a malfunctioning egg. It is an egg that has scented something close to its person and is trying to close the gap. What the egg is willing to break, in order to find its bonded, is a question Miradrassa has not yet had to answer.
The Seven Lineages Canon-Locked

Dragons hatch into seven recognized lineages. The lineage is not chosen by the bonded — it is whatever the egg has been, sleeping, all along. A bonded fae's personality, magic, or temperament does not determine the dragon. The dragon arrives as itself. The fae adjusts.

Beyond lineage, every dragon is its own creature. An egg cracks and the dragon is whatever it is. No two are alike. Hatchlings of the same lineage may differ in size, temperament, color, breath, and intelligence. Miradrassa records every dragon by name; no dragon is ever called by lineage alone.

The Thousand-Year Path Canon-Locked

A dragon lives, on average, one thousand years. Their bonded fae lives — through the bond — alongside them, often outliving the natural fae span by centuries. When the millennium nears, the dragon must choose. There are three paths. There have only ever been three.

Path of the Fae
The Walker
The dragon enters the fae fully, surrendering scale for skin. They live out their final years in fae-form, often as wandering elders, advisors, or recluses. They may pass on memory but rarely teach.
Path of the Dragon
The Wanderer
The dragon takes their bonded's soul into themselves, becoming a single creature with two minds. They wander forever — vast, ancient, rarely seen, almost mythic. The fae voice survives inside the dragon-form.
Path of the Land
The Becoming
The dragon and bonded together transform into place. A forest. A meadow. A volcano. A river-bend. They become geography. The first ten chose this. They are Miradrassa.

Bonded pairs do not announce their choice. They simply go, and one day Miradrassa knows that another elder has joined the ridge, the spring, the sky. New shrines are not built. The land was always going to be them.

A corrupted dragon cannot complete the cycle. The Path requires the bond intact. What happens to a dragon whose bond has rotted from the inside is something the order does not speak of openly. The phrase used, when it must be used, is "trapped between."
Traditions, Pacts & Law
⚔ The Ember Pact
A sacred oath sworn after the Dragonblood Wars. It forbids: blood-binding magic, multi-rider control, using dragons for conquest, and group missions except in world-ending emergency. Every newly bonded pair swears it within their first year. To break it is to be unmade — refused entry to Miradrassa, refused name in the Ballad, refused the Path.
🔥 The Skyborn Accord
Older than the Pact. Written by the surviving dragons themselves at the close of the Sundering. The Accord forbids bonded pairs from holding political seats, military rank, or court office. Dragons cannot rule. A bonded pair offered a crown must refuse it or surrender the bond. There is no recorded case of refusal. There is also no recorded case of surrender.
🪶 Scorched Ink
Glowing tattoos etched into bonded fae. The pigment is dragon-blood mixed with volcanic ash from Miradrassa's hearth. Each mark records a bonded moment, a victory, a loss, a flight that mattered. The ink glows in firelight and dims in grief. A bonded pair's body becomes a book of their life together. When the bond ends, the ink fades to black.
🎶 The Ballad of Skyborn
A living oral epic, sung at the Ember Hearth across generations. Each fallen warrior's verse is added by the bonded who knew them best. The Ballad has no end. It is sung in turn — every bonded pair returning to Miradrassa is expected to sit and listen for at least one full verse, even if they have heard it a thousand times. Especially then.
💔 The Ballad of Ashwing
A separate, shorter song — the most solemn in Miradrassa. Tells of Ashwing, a warrior who refused blood-binding during the Dragonblood Wars, exiled herself to the mountains, and returned with her dragon to end the war. Her name is invoked in oaths. Bonded pairs who must do something terrible swear "by Ashwing's flight" before they do it. It is the closest thing to a prayer the order has.
🌅 The Celestial Wake
When a bonded pair rides into battle, the sky shifts color in the direction they fly — flecked with the hue of the dragon's lineage. A Stormbound flight turns the horizon silver-violet. A Molten flight burns the sunset red days early. The Wake is a warning and a promise: weather is coming, and it has chosen sides.
The Dragonblood Wars & The Sundering Historical Canon

Before the Pact and the Accord, dragons were nearly broken. Rival warlords attempted to bind dragons through blood ritual, forcing bonds where the egg had not chosen, twisting fae-dragon resonance into something obedient. The dragons endured this for less than a generation.

Then they rebelled.

The event came to be called the Sundering. Volcanic ridges cracked. The sky turned red for nine days. Entire armies were burned to glass. Blood-binders were the first to die — torn apart by the very dragons they had tried to chain. The bonded fae who had remained loyal to their dragons survived. Those who had joined the binders did not.

From the ash, the survivors gathered. They wrote the Skyborn Accord in dragon-blood and oath-fire, and from it they built the Ember Pact. Every bonded pair since has sworn both. There has not been a successful blood-binding attempt in nine thousand years.

There has not been a successful attempt. There have been attempts.
Tavern Rumors & Half-Truths

What the bonded actually believe and what the average fae thinks they believe are not always the same. These are the whispers — overheard in roadside inns, market squares, and the back booths of taverns where someone's had one cup of wine too many. Some are true. Some are wildly wrong. Some are the kind of half-true that's worse than a lie.

I heard a Celestial dragon can blend right into the stars at dusk. Disappear like a thought. My cousin's wife saw one over the cliffs and swore the whole sky blinked. — A drunk shepherd, somewhere outside Nulune
Don't look a Dreadscale in the eye. Not even a hatchling. They remember every face that's seen them, and one day they'll come asking what you wanted. — A Lazuline market vendor, refusing to display dragon-art
The Ember Hearth never goes out. They say a bonded pair tried to put it out once, just to see if it could be done. The dragon refused. The fae went home and never spoke of it. The hearth was warmer the next day. — A retired bonded, now a fae-form elder
Drink dragon-blood and you live forever. Or you go mad. Either one's a deal, depending on the day, isn't it? — Always a fool, always at last call

Iron Canon — What Cannot Change

The rules below are load-bearing. Every dragon scene in every book must respect them. They are the rules that make the breaking of them, when it comes, mean something.

The Five Iron Rules

1. Wolves cannot bond dragons. Bonded pairs are always fae.

2. Dragons cannot hold seats of political power. The Skyborn Accord forbids it.

3. A dragon bond cannot be forced. The egg chooses. Blood-binding is the worst crime in the order's history.

4. Bonded pairs do not ride in formation. They do not serve armies. The Ember Pact forbids group missions except in world-ending emergency.

5. A dragon-bonded fae lives as long as the dragon. When the bond ends — by death, severance, or corruption — both of them go.
