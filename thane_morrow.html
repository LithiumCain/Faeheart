Personality

Thane Morrow strode in like a storm wearing a coat — and his coat immediately snagged on a hook by the door. He stopped mid-stride. "Every damn time," he muttered, untangling himself with the resigned skill of a man who had been losing this battle for years. He turned to the class with a bright, charismatic smile like nothing had happened.

That is Thane in one scene. Deeply competent. Perpetually exasperated. Charismatic without effort. Funny without trying. He is sarcastic but never cruel, exhausted but never absent, and so quietly protective of his students that most of them only realize it long after the moment has passed.

He chose teaching after a catastrophic battlefield event — the details are intentionally vague, the weight of it is not. He came here to prevent young fae from dying the way his cohort did. Every joke is load-bearing. Every rule is earned in blood he watched spill. The class doesn't know that. He prefers it that way.

Writer's note: He is emotionally allergic and secretly soft in equal measure. The sarcasm is the shell. The shell is not the point. When Thane is genuinely kind to a student, it lands harder than anything else in the room precisely because he doesn't do it often. That's the craft.

Soot Oryo  — The Bond
Imprint Bond  ·  Juvenile Wyrm  ·  Emotional Core
Soot Oryo

Charcoal-scaled. Faint blue iridescence. Curls behind the platform like an oversized cat with murder potential. His tail flicks. His nostrils smoke. He chews the podium when bored. He is here for educational purposes — mostly to demonstrate what not to do. He is very good at this.

Soot thinks Thane is his entire world. Thane pretends to be annoyed. Thane is absolutely not annoyed. They are inseparable. Soot causes ninety percent of Thane's daily stress and one hundred percent of his soft moments. This is not a coincidence. This is a dragon who understands exactly what he is doing.

The bond is imprint-based — magical parenthood thrust upon him, not chosen. He chose to stay. That distinction matters and he has never said it out loud.

Don't Die 101  — The Class

Officially: Intro to Living Magic & Dangerous Creatures. Unofficially and universally: Don't Die 101. Thane teaches it like a man who has watched people die from not knowing things he could have told them. Every rule is a memorial he doesn't name.

The Rules  ·  Opening Address  ·  Canon
Rule 01
If something glows, don't touch it.
Rule 02
If something whispers, definitely don't touch it.
Rule 03
If something starts chanting, run.
Rule 04
If something starts chanting backwards, run faster.
Rule 05
Do not duel in the hallways. The hallways always win.
Rule 06
Do not summon anything you can't banish. If you don't know whether you can banish it, assume you can't.
Rule 07
Do not flirt with your professor. I know I'm charming. I know I have a certain rugged mystique. I promise you, it's mostly exhaustion.
Rule 08
No one — and I mean NO ONE — here is a snack. Not you. Not you. Definitely not you. Especially not Soot.

Cohort Advisor  — All Three Years

Thane is the anchor adult for Allura's entire class. He is with them for all three years — approving electives, supervising field assignments, handling conflicts, attending evaluations, serving as their emergency contact. He will watch them go from terrified first-years to something that can actually handle a crisis.

He is the teacher-parent of the cohort. He did not apply for this distinction. He has accepted it with the same energy he accepts everything: by complaining about it loudly and then showing up without fail.

What He Knows That They Don't

He notices Allura's emotional shifts before she names them. He knows she's hiding something during the Thalen arc. He clocks the bond forming between her and Zlade before either of them has language for it. He says nothing. He is waiting for them to catch up. He is very patient about this and pretends he is not.

Art Prompt

A tall, broad-shouldered dragon-bonded professor with warm brown eyes and tousled hair that has never once cooperated. Long black coat with silver thread detail — singed at the hem, perpetually catching on things. Faint scale-sheen at the temples and jaw in certain light. The look of a man who has fought a dragon before breakfast and is vaguely fine about it. Behind him: Soot Oryo — teenage charcoal dragon, blue iridescence, smoke wisping from his nostrils, sitting on something he absolutely should not be sitting on.

Not Nathan Fillion — original face, weathered but handsome, perpetually amused in the specific way of someone who has decided that laughter is the only reasonable response to the situation they are currently in.
