The Ears — Lycari Canon In faecoat form, wolf ears are always present. Always. They are not glamoured, not hidden, not suppressed. They are part of what the Lycari are — the one constant between forms. For Reign, his ears are controlled with the same iron discipline he applies to everything else. Flat in anger or grief. Perked forward in alertness. The tell he can't quite silence: they angle toward Allura before he's consciously aware she's near. She hasn't noticed yet.

Furcoat Form Canon
Dark Markings
Inky black across the back, shoulders, flanks, face, and ears. Full black muzzle. Black ears. The darkness is total — not grey, not dark brown. Black like deep water at night.
Cream Markings
Cream and beige on all four legs, face panels, and shoulders — like he's wearing something ancient and ceremonial. A diamond marking on the forehead. White teeth, moon-bright.
Distinguishing Marks
Forehead
Diamond marking — pale against dark fur. Visible at distance. Unmistakable up close.
Size
Larger than other wolves. Noticeably. Allura clocks him across a treeline by size alone before she sees his markings.
Movement
Quiet for something that large. Nails click on cobblestones — that sound is a tell. In the forest: near-silent.
Glamoured form
Same coloring, same markings, but softer — "more approachable, harmless looking enough to be ignored by most fae." Still unmistakably large. Still him.
When Allura watches the pack run, she clocks three large wolves and identifies him last — the cream-and-dark one who looks right at her and flashes gold. That eye flash is the confirmation. Use it as a recurring tell whenever the wolf is close to the surface in faecoat too.
Everyday Style
The Reference Jensen Ackles energy — the guy who looks effortlessly good because he's genuinely comfortable, not performing. Earthy tones, warm neutrals, the occasional splash of color that surprises you. A little nerdy warmth woven in. Not Harvey Specter. Not a suit-and-tie man. Someone who could walk into a forest or a casual dinner and look exactly right for both.

Silhouette
Nothing loose enough to catch in a shift. Fitted through the shoulders and chest — his build does the talking. Simple cuts, nothing fussy. He didn't think about it. It still works.
Layers
Usually. A henley or simple top underneath, something over it — a jacket, an open shirt, a worn layer he can move in. The layering is functional, not styled. It ends up looking styled anyway.
Signature
Boots, always. Dark trousers that fit well. The occasional piece with unexpected detail — a subtle texture, a small embellishment — that you only notice when you're standing close. Which most people don't get to do.
The nerd thread
Small, specific details that read as curated if you're paying attention. A pin, a particular book tucked under his arm, something that doesn't quite fit the apex predator image — and is therefore exactly him.
What he avoids
Anything that announces effort. Anything stiff. Anything that would slow a shift. He will never be caught in something he can't move in.
His style secret: the warmth. Earth tones on warm tan skin with golden-brown hair creates a cohesive naturalness that reads as effortless. He looks like he grew out of the forest and just happens to be wearing clothes. That's right. That's canon.
Formal / Ball Look
The Lazuline Ball — Suit Canon
Base color
Deep charcoal — not quite black, not grey. The color of storm clouds before they break. It reads as black across a ballroom. Up close, it has depth.
Silhouette
Perfectly tailored through the shoulders — his build is the statement. Not boxy, not slim-cut vanity. Structured enough to look intentional, relaxed enough that he can still move. Kaelith probably had a hand in this.
The one detail
Subtle wolf-pack symbolism worked into the lapel or collar — a small engraved clasp, a fine embroidered line, something you'd only see if you were close and looking. Understated. He doesn't announce what he is. The detail is for those who already know.
Underneath
Deep charcoal or near-black underneath — no contrast break. The whole look reads as one continuous dark form. He disappears into the room until he doesn't.
Hair
The curls are present but settled — slightly more composed than everyday, still him. Kaelith adjusted it. He let her. He'd deny caring about this.
At the dance
He moves better in this suit than he should. He's been taught. His wolf knows how to move a body. The combination is devastating.
The contrast that matters at the ball: Allura is light — gold, lavender, stardust. Reign is dark — charcoal, shadow, quiet menace in a suit. When they stand together on the dance floor, the visual is already telling the story. Don't over-describe it. Let the colors do it.
Blood Jewelry — The Craft
How it works A single claw drawn across his palm. Blood rises in floating droplets — not falling, suspended, shimmering garnet-red in the air. He shapes them with slow, practiced hand movements, the rhythm something between ritual and muscle memory. When he pulls the tether tight, they harden into glasslike jewels — cool to the touch, catching light from inside as if the blood remembers being warm.
His Own — Ear Cuff & Neck Piece
Deliberately understated. He made himself something elegant but small — an ear cuff that follows the curve of cartilage, thin as a whisper, with a single garnet drop. The neck piece is a fine dark chain with one shaped jewel at the hollow of his throat, barely visible above his collar. Nothing that announces itself.
He made these last and smallest. "Who was he to wear a crown, even to a formal event?" The restraint in what he crafted for himself says everything about who he thinks he is in Book 1. The blood remembers the wound even when he won't.
Kaelith's — Ruby Circlet
A delicate arcing circlet of fused ruby droplets, each one shaped individually before being set. A single teardrop gem suspended at the center, meant to rest just between her brows. Smaller droplets form a constellation trail across her cheekbones — they follow the sharp, wolfish angles of her face like they were mapped to her specifically. Because they were.
He knows her face better than she knows it. The placement is exact. He didn't measure. He's been looking at her his whole life.
Riven's — Crescent Moon Piece
A forehead piece shaped as a crescent moon, radiating outward in tiny star-shaped shards — each one a frozen tear of his blood. The whole piece glows faintly, as if it caught invisible moonlight and kept it. It should look ridiculous. On Riven, it looks like it was always supposed to exist.
Riven will wear it like he was born in it. Reign knew he would. That's why he made it this way.
Canon Note — The Blood Magic The jewels harden into glass but they don't go cold immediately — there's a warmth in them that fades slowly, like they're mourning being blood. When Allura eventually learns what these pieces are made of, that detail matters. He gave pieces of himself to the people he loves. Literally. And then made himself something small.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Reign dresses like Someone who grew up in the wilds and learned to exist in civilization without being shaped by it. His clothes are warm and natural because he is. He doesn't perform belonging anywhere — not in Nulune, not at the University, not at a formal ball. He simply arrives and the space adjusts. That is, in itself, a kind of power most fae spend their whole lives trying to manufacture.
The contrast that defines him Big but quiet. Dark clothing, warm coloring. Apex predator who gives wounded wolves blood-forged gifts and then makes himself something small. He is the storm that walks softly. His style, like everything about him, is a contradiction that resolves into something true.
