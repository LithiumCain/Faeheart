Canon Note — The Bandage There is always a bandage somewhere on Calder's person. Forearm. Knuckles. Eyebrow. Calf. Students have started a running tally. Nobody has ever seen him apply one — they simply appear between classes, each one telling a story he will happily recount if asked. He is asked constantly. He tells constantly. Most of the stories are true.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) He strolls onto the sparring field like he's arriving at a cookout. Tall, rangy, sandy-blond hair that has never met a comb it respected. Sun-weathered warm tan skin, freckled and happily scarred. Bright storm-blue eyes that crinkle at the corners before they've even registered what's funny. A bandage on his knuckles today, a different one tomorrow. He says "wow" at least six times per class. He also corrects footwork with terrifying precision between "wow"s. Students learn quickly: the chaos is the teaching method.
Neutral / third person shorthand He looked like he wrestled magical creatures for fun, talked like a man narrating his own weather report, and taught like someone who'd personally survived every mistake his students were about to make.
Calder's voice is enthusiastic chaos wrapped around genuine expertise. Owen Wilson cadence, Woody Harrelson energy. He'll say "wow" to buy himself half a second to think — and then deliver a combat correction that saves a student's life. When Reign's daggers explode, Calder's response is: "WOW. Look at that! Blood daggers! From a wolf! That's… that's incredible. Terrifying. But incredible. We should definitely study him. Not in a creepy way. In a… mildly concerning academic way." He takes Reign because he thinks it's cool — and because Reign might accidentally kill someone if left unsupervised.

Ceremony. Neckties. Anything crisp. He once wore a formal coat to a faculty dinner and has not been invited back — on purpose, by himself.
Field Expedition Gear
Silhouette
Layered, weatherproof, buckles everywhere. A long duster that's been set on fire at least once. Kit bag slung across his chest that jingles like it contains mysteries.
Details
Storm-rune tattoos visible along his forearms, lit faintly when weather shifts. A compass on a chain that does not point north and he refuses to fix it.
Energy
Students report feeling 30% more likely to survive the wilderness when he's leading the expedition. They are correct.
Calder's wardrobe says come hike with me, you will be fine, probably. The bandage says I am personally intimate with every mistake you are about to make. Both are true simultaneously. Both are the teaching method.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Calder dresses like He dresses like a man who has never once cared what he looks like and has somehow still pulled it off for forty years. Storm blue, wheat gold, weathered tan — a palette from the sky above a sparring field. His wardrobe is pragmatic joy. You could not dress him up if you tried. Several faculty have tried.
The teacher he is Chaotic, enthusiastic, absolutely not qualified on paper — and somehow perfect. He loves weird things. He loves his students. He claps Reign on the shoulder after the blood-dagger incident and says "Dibs on training him! I get him Tuesdays." Students who survive his first expedition would die for him by their second. He would, obviously, die for them first.
