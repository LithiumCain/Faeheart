Wings — The Shimmer

Iridescent. Translucent. Long. They trail light like a living aurora — and they crackle when he's pleased, when he's making a point, or when the world needs to pay attention. Students call it "the shimmer." He calls it "shimmer." It has become, inexplicably, a full sentence.

Personality

Banister Whitis operates on a frequency slightly to the left of everyone else. He says things that sound like nonsense and turn out to be prophecy. He wanders campus at odd hours. His chaos is intentional, not random — every apparently offhand comment lands somewhere specific, usually in the exact place it was needed most.

He gives students the advice they need, never the advice they want. He is deeply kind beneath the cosmic static. He is a little unhinged in a way that is somehow comforting rather than alarming. He has "I know more than I'm saying" energy in every interaction, and he never denies it.

Half the faculty adore him. The other half are afraid of him. Most are both. He is aware of all three camps and finds each of them entirely reasonable.

"You were brought here because something in you stirred. A spark. A whisper. A pull you couldn't name. Your parents didn't tell you everything. That's alright. Secrets are just stories waiting for the right moment."

Writer's note: He is not comic relief — he is cosmic relief. The distinction matters. His humor is not there to deflate tension. It is there to reframe it. When Banister laughs, something shifts. That's the point.

Magic & Abilities

Banister is extremely powerful and rarely shows it. His magic feels like starlight, wind, and intuition — not sharp or violent, but vast. It does not announce itself. It has already arrived.

The Shimmer  — Canon Breakdown

At the Opening Convergence Ceremony, Banister uses "shimmer" as living punctuation — a pause, a revelation, a signal. Each one lands differently. His wings crackle on each iteration. The sequence below is from the manuscript.

Shimmer 1
Addressed to the Wolven students. Acknowledging instinct and the moon. Soft. Welcoming.
Shimmer 2
Addressed to the Fae students who forgot their magic. Gentle. Forgiving. "That's alright. Magic didn't forget you."
Shimmer 3
Addressed to the Owlkin. The sky and the riddles. Slightly unsettling because they blink in unison.
Shimmer 4
Addressed to the dragon eggs. "Haven't quite hatched yet. That's alright. We're patient." One of the eggs hums back.
Final Shimmer
Full wing flare. Brilliant. Crackling. Breathtaking. The crowd laughs. Then he introduces Thane and steps aside with a flourish.

"Shimmer on, children."

Art Prompt

A tall, loose-limbed headmaster with warm sun-touched skin and iridescent wings that trail light like a living aurora. Wind-tousled hair — it looks this way indoors too. Eyes that shift between starlight and quiet amusement. Clothing that is somehow slightly askew and somehow perfect. Constellation-stitched coat. He looks like someone who has never once rushed, and has never needed to. He is not performing ease — he simply lives there.

Not Matthew McConaughey — original face, golden-toned and ageless, the kind of features that don't track to any specific number of years. He could be forty. He could be four hundred. The eyes are the tell: they have seen more than they admit, and they find it interesting.
