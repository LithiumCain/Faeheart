Canon Note — The Talon One large raptor talon on her right foot that clicks when she walks on stone. Students learn to recognize the sound before they see her. When she's trying to be silent, she isn't — she just chooses not to be heard.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (Allura's POV) A hurricane arrives in a ballroom and calls it fashion. Soft waves pinned with feathers that catch lanternlight like they were woven by moonlight itself. Silver-blue eyes that flick to you and see you in about half a second. Warm brown skin with a faint moonlit sheen along her collarbone, like her skin remembers the sky. She doesn't walk into a room — she decides the room, and then occupies it.
Neutral / third person shorthand She was the kind of beautiful that made other people's posture improve. Feathers in her hair, truth in her eyes, and a grin that promised she already knew your secrets.
When writing Renne, lead with presence before appearance. She announces herself — then the description catches up. Feathers and silver-blue are her anchor images. Let the talon click land on stone at least once per scene she walks through.
Everyday Style
Campus / General Wear
Silhouette
Layered, flowing, unapologetically feminine. Nothing boxy. Pieces that move when she moves.
Palette
Moon silverWarm goldMidnight navyIvory
Fabrics
Silks, sheer layers, lightweight wools. Anything that flutters. She wants fabric that behaves like feathers whether or not she's in shifter form.
Signature pieces
Feather earrings (real plumage, family-blessed). Gold + moonstone jewelry, always layered. Subtle owl-motif clasps. A layer she shrugs off dramatically at least once a day.
What she avoids
Anything structured or boring. If it's not in motion, it's not Renne.
Field / Study Days
Silhouette
Fitted under, flowing over. Leggings or fitted trousers, loose layered top, short structured cloak. Feathers become functional.
Details
Boots she can run in. Braided hair instead of loose waves. Talon visible. Gold glints at the throat and wrists — she doesn't leave them behind.
Energy
She looks like she came from the ballroom and forgot to change. She didn't. She just doesn't see the difference.
Renne's style secret is contrast in motion. A silk layer over leggings. Moonstone at the throat with scuffed boots. She doesn't match — she curates.
Formal / Ball Look
The Ball Gown — Scene Canon
Silhouette
Regal, iridescent, celestial. Plucked feathers woven directly into the fabric — a dress that is also plumage, plumage that is also a dress. Column through the torso, fuller at the hem so it moves like wingbeats.
Color story
Silver to gold, shifting with every breath. Not a gradient — a response. The fabric changes value depending on how she moves. Indoors it reads silver. Toward lanternlight it flashes gold.
Fabric
Woven Strixian feathers layered over fine silk. Lightweight. Catches air. Silent, of course — it's owl plumage.
Embellishment
Feathers alone. No crystals, no beading, nothing competing. The plumage is the statement.
Headdress
A feathered crest arching from her crown like a celestial diadem. Aweinspiring and unmistakably her. The headdress is the thesis statement of the whole look.
Arms / neckline
Neckline bares the throat and collarbone. Arms exposed so the subtle feathering along her forearms is visible. The gown is made to show you what she is without telling you.
Ball — Hair & Accessories
Hair
Swept up beneath the headdress. Loose tendrils. Moonstone pins.
Jewelry
Gold hoops. Layered moonstone chains. Feather-dagger earrings that graze her shoulders when she turns her head.
Hands
Bare. Rings stacked at two fingers — family rings, not decoration.
Shoes
Gold, low-heeled, built for dancing and chaos. She has plans for both.
The Entrance — Ball Scene Canon Renne's ball entrance in Book 1 is a co-canon moment with Allura's. She hooks Reign's arm without permission, calls him Fluffy, and cuts clean through his feral trance with one line. The dress shifts silver-to-gold as she pulls him through the crowd. This is the moment she establishes herself as the character who owns every room she enters — and the only person Reign reliably obeys.
When writing Renne in motion at the ball, the dress breathes. Each inhale brightens it toward gold, each exhale cools it toward silver. Let the palette of the room flicker as she passes.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Renne dresses like She dresses like someone who's been paying attention to every ballroom for ten years and decided to win. Feathers as fashion statement, not gimmick. Silver and gold not as opposites but as weather she wears. The wardrobe of a woman who knows she's a predator and dresses the part — elegantly.
The contrast that defines her Soft waves and a raptor's talon. Silk layers and a predator's eye. Warmth in her voice and a stare that could pin a liar to the wall. Renne is the friend who hypes you up, calls you out, and saves your life — sometimes in that order.
