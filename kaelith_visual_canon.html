Eye Canon — Lycari Alpha System
Species Canon — Blue Eyes & Gold Flash All Lycari have blue eyes in faecoat form. The gold flash is reserved for alphas only — it surfaces when facing another alpha, or in rare cases, in the presence of a mate bond. It is involuntary. It is a tell. It is one of the ways Lycari rank is read without words.
Kaelith — Default
Storm blue. Grey-blue, still, watchful. Gives nothing away. The eye of the storm. No gold — she is not an alpha.
Alpha Flash — Gold
Reign only. Involuntary. Surfaces near other alphas or near Allura. A tell he cannot fully control. Kaelith has clocked it. She hasn't said anything yet.
The Rule
Blue eyes = Lycari. Gold flash = alpha status surfacing. In a room full of fae who don't know wolf lore, it means nothing. To another Lycari, it means everything.
Furcoat Form Canon
Coloring
Pure black. No variation, no secondary markings, no softening. In the treeline at night she is invisible until she moves. In daylight she reads as shadow given teeth. Her furcoat matches her faecoat with the same precision she applies to everything else — she chose this, or it chose her. Either way, it's right.
Wolf Form Notes
Size
Smaller than Reign and Riven in furcoat — but the pack isn't ranked by size. Her movements are the most efficient of the three. Nothing wasted.
Movement
Where Reign moves quiet and Riven moves fast, Kaelith moves exact. You don't hear her coming. You don't see her until she's already there. That is the point.
Contrast
All black against any terrain. She's the hardest to spot and the easiest to underestimate. Both are intentional. Both have kept her alive.
Her Ears — The Tell She Hates Kaelith's ears are her most expressive feature and she resents it. She has iron control over her face, her voice, her posture. Her ears betray her anyway. They go flat when she's truly angry — not irritated, not exasperated, but the cold kind of angry that precedes action. The one time they've been seen fully forward, alert, ears up: when Riven is in genuine danger. She'd like everyone to forget that happened.
Everyday Style — Faecoat
The Reference Morticia Addams elegance — but she didn't get the memo about staying monochrome, and honestly she's better for it. High contrast is her foundation: the dark base, the sharp lines, the way she wears structure like armor. Then something soft appears somewhere — a dusty rose detail, a blush accessory, one unexpected feminine note — and it reframes everything. She's not cold. She's precise. There's a difference.
The Base
Black, deep charcoal, midnight navy, ivory white. High contrast always. The foundation never changes.

The Surprise
Dusty rose. Blush. A soft warm note that shouldn't work against all that dark — and lands harder because of it.
Campus / General Wear
Palette

Silhouette
Fitted and structured — nothing loose, nothing that creates drag. She dresses like someone who might need to move without warning, but does it elegantly. Clean lines. Deliberate cuts.
The contrast
She will wear an all-black fitted ensemble with one blush accessory — a pin, a ribbon, a single soft detail — and it will read as more striking than anyone in the room who tried harder. The softness is the statement.
Silhouette note
High necklines or precise collar details. Nothing that reads as careless. She looks assembled, always — not overdressed, just exact.
Signature
One soft piece per outfit. Always unexpected. Never accidental. She knows exactly what she's doing when she adds the blush. She just won't explain it.
What she avoids
Excess. Anything that rustles. Anything that announces her arrival before she's ready to be announced. Jewelry that catches on things. Riven's approach to getting dressed, as a general principle.
The dusty rose against deep black reads as softness — which is exactly why she uses it. People look at the blush and relax. That moment of relaxation is information. She is always collecting information.
Formal / Ball Look
The Lazuline Ball — Canon
Base
Deep black or midnight — the high-contrast foundation holds even at formal events. She doesn't abandon her aesthetic for a party. The party adjusts.
The pop
At the ball, the blush appears in the gown itself — perhaps a panel, a sash, a layer of dusty rose tulle beneath black. Soft against severe. The combination is striking in the candlelight in a way that sneaks up on you.
Reign's circlet
Ruby droplets fused into delicate arcs, a teardrop gem between her brows, smaller drops forming a constellation across her cheekbones — mapped to the sharp, wolfish angles of her face. Reign made it to fit her specifically. It shows. She knows it shows. She wore it anyway.
Hair at ball
Down, with intention — the long dark waves loose against black fabric is its own kind of statement. She usually keeps it back. When she doesn't, people notice the difference.
Movement
She dances the way she does everything — with precision that reads as effortless. No wasted motion. No stumbled steps. She learned because it was useful. She does it beautifully because that's who she is.
At the ball, the three of them together: Reign in charcoal shadow, Riven in warm gold, Kaelith in black and blush. They read as a unit before you know they are one. The contrast between them is the visual language of the pack — dark, light, and the precise thing standing between them.
