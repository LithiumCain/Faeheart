Canon Note — The Wind-Chime She keeps a tiny wind-chime charm on her belt that jingles when she's excited — which is often. Students know she's about to say something brilliant when the jingle starts. Students know she's about to grade something brutally when the jingle stops.
Prose-Ready Description
First impression (student POV) Bright-eyed and small enough that half the class assumes she's a TA on the first day. Warm brown skin with a faint rain-silver shimmer along her jaw. Soft waves, rain-silver streaks, stray pieces that never quite stay pinned. Silver-blue eyes that crinkle when she laughs. She talks with her hands, laughs easily, assigns three pages of field notes without blinking, and grades harder than Goldbeak. Ask anyone. She'll tell you herself.
Neutral / third person shorthand She looked like a student and taught like a storm-front. Students underestimated her exactly once.
Rainfeather is deceptively strict. The energy is warm and bright — and the rubric is merciless. Her gift is explaining complex concepts in simple metaphors that students remember for years. She takes her class outside "because the sky feels right today." It always feels right. She reads the sky correctly every single time.

What she avoids
Heavy robes. Anything she can't hike in. Dark colors — they don't match the sky.
Her office is full of plants she forgets to water that thrive anyway. She collects shiny rocks on field days and lines her windowsill with them. The rocks catch light. The plants don't care. Everyone is, somehow, fine.
Style Identity — The Through Line
Who Rainfeather dresses like She dresses like a young scholar who never stopped being delighted by the sky. Rain-silver, cloud white, storm blue — a palette built from weather she loves. Her wardrobe is kinetic, warm, and entirely uninterested in intimidating anyone. That's the trap.
The teacher she is Warm. Approachable. Absolutely merciless with late assignments. She grades harder than Goldbeak and thinks that's funny. Students adore her and fear her, which is exactly where she wants them.
